# Harmonic Fields: All Major and Minor Keys

A **harmonic field** (also called *diatonic field* or *key harmonization*) is the set of seven chords built by stacking thirds on each degree of a scale. Each chord uses only notes from that scale, giving every key its characteristic harmonic palette.

---

## Chord Quality Pattern

| Degree | Roman Numeral | Major Key Quality | Natural Minor Key Quality |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| 1st    | I / i        | Major             | Minor                    |
| 2nd    | ii / ii┬░     | Minor             | Diminished               |
| 3rd    | III / έβφIII   | Minor             | Major                    |
| 4th    | IV / iv      | Major             | Minor                    |
| 5th    | V / v        | Major             | Minor                    |
| 6th    | vi / έβφVI     | Minor             | Major                    |
| 7th    | vii┬░ / έβφVII  | Diminished        | Major                    |

> **Harmonic Minor** raises the 7th degree, turning vέΗΤV (major) and έβφVIIέΗΤvii┬░ (diminished), creating a leading tone for stronger resolution.

## Scale Formulas Reference

### Pentatonic Scales
| Scale | Formula (intervals) | Degrees omitted from parent scale |
|-------|--------------------|------------------------------------|
| Major Pentatonic | 1 έΑΥ 2 έΑΥ 3 έΑΥ 5 έΑΥ 6 | Omits 4th and 7th |
| Minor Pentatonic | 1 έΑΥ έβφ3 έΑΥ 4 έΑΥ 5 έΑΥ έβφ7 | Omits 2nd and 6th |

### Blues Scales
| Scale | Formula (intervals) | Addition over pentatonic |
|-------|--------------------|------------------------------------|
| Major Blues | 1 έΑΥ 2 έΑΥ έβφ3 έΑΥ 3 έΑΥ 5 έΑΥ 6 | Adds έβφ3 (blue note) to major pentatonic |
| Minor Blues | 1 έΑΥ έβφ3 έΑΥ 4 έΑΥ έβφ5 έΑΥ 5 έΑΥ έβφ7 | Adds έβφ5 (blue note) to minor pentatonic |

> The **blue note** (έβφ3 in major blues, έβφ5 in minor blues) is the characteristic tension note that gives the blues its expressive, bending quality.
